Insights from Earnings Calls

Key Technology Narratives

The Gate-All-Around (GAA) Inflection

The adoption of Gate-All-Around (GAA) transistor architectures represents a pivotal technological inflection point, significantly enhancing Applied Materials' market opportunities within the memory sector, particularly in leading-edge foundry-logic. This shift from FinFET to GAA is a direct response to the industry's imperative to achieve substantial improvements in energy efficiency for advanced AI chips, with a goal of driving a 10,000 times improvement in performance-per-watt over the next 15 years. Applied Materials is strategically positioned to capitalise on this transition, with its served available market (SAM) for the transistor module projected to grow from approximately $6 billion to $7 billion for every 100,000 wafer starts per month of capacity as GAA nodes transition to high-volume manufacturing. The company anticipates capturing over 50% of the process equipment spending for these advanced transistor steps, a notable increase from the mid-to-high 40% range seen with FinFET generation fabs. When considering the broader impact, including Backside Power Delivery (BPD), Applied's total available market is expected to grow by more than 15% to around $14 billion for every 100,000 wafer starts-per-month of capacity. This expansion is expected to translate into Applied's related revenues growing in the 30% range for the equivalent wafer fab capacity with the shift to GAA and BPD. The financial impact of GAA is increasingly evident in Applied Materials' results. The company generated more than $2.5 billion of revenue from GAA nodes in fiscal year 2024 (FY24), with projections for these revenues to approximately double in fiscal year 2025 (FY25). This growth is further supported by the rapid adoption of innovative solutions such as the Sym3 Magnum etch system, which has demonstrated strong momentum in GAA applications and generated more than $1.2 billion of revenue since its launch in February 2024. Additionally, Applied's Cold Field Emission (CFE) eBeam technology, also showing strong momentum in GAA, contributed to record revenues for its Process Diagnostics and Control business in Q2 Fiscal 2025. The Semiconductor Systems segment reported 20% year-over-year growth in foundry-logic revenue in Q1 Fiscal 2025, partially attributed to GAA ramps, and continued strong foundry-logic sales driven by customer investments for GAA nodes in Q2 Fiscal 2025. In Q3 Fiscal 2025, the metal deposition business, bolstered by leading-edge foundry strength, achieved nearly $1.2 billion in revenue, securing its first wins in Moly deposition for critical device performance applications. The full ramp-up of these GAA nodes, particularly with BPD, is anticipated to yield multiple points of market share gains for Applied Materials in the second half of 2026 and 2027.

Powering the AI-Driven Memory Revolution

Applied Materials' earnings reports reveal a dynamic and evolving memory market, increasingly shaped by the demands of artificial intelligence (AI) and driven by significant materials engineering advancements. The company consistently highlights its pivotal role in enabling these shifts through its broad portfolio of solutions. The High-Bandwidth Memory (HBM) segment stands out as a primary growth driver, central to AI datacentres due to its high performance and energy efficiency. In fiscal year 2023, HBM constituted approximately 5% of DRAM output but was projected to grow at a 50% compound annual growth rate in the years that followed. The larger die size of HBM, more than double that of standard DRAM, necessitates over twice the fab capacity for equivalent chip volume, further boosting equipment demand. Applied Materials' HBM packaging revenue demonstrated substantial growth, initially expected to be four times larger in fiscal year 2024 compared to 2023, reaching almost $500 million, then revised upwards to potentially six times higher, exceeding $600 million. By fiscal year 2024, HBM packaging revenues actually surpassed $700 million. This growth trajectory continued into fiscal year 2025, with advanced DRAM customer revenues, including HBM, expected to grow more than 40%. The overall advanced packaging product portfolio, encompassing HBM, generated nearly $1.7 billion in fiscal year 2024, tripling in the preceding four years, with potential to double again in the coming years due to increased heterogeneous integration. This segment is further projected to exceed $3 billion in the next few years. Beyond HBM, the broader DRAM market is undergoing significant technological inflections that enhance Applied Materials' market opportunity. The shift from 6F2 to 4F2 (vertical transistor architectures) in DRAM is highly materials-enabled, anticipated to increase Applied Materials' market opportunity by approximately 10%. The subsequent transition to 3D DRAM is expected to further expand the addressable market by an additional 15%, with Applied Materials positioned to achieve over 50% market share in these new architectures. The company has already gained significant DRAM market share, reporting more than a 10-point increase over the past decade and their DRAM revenues in 2023 were larger than their two closest process equipment peers combined. Overall, the company's "inflection-focused innovation strategy" and its leadership in materials engineering solutions are key to its continued outperformance in the memory market.

Q3 Record Highs and a Cautious Q4 Outlook

China Headwinds: The Core of the Muted Forecast

Applied Materials has forecasted a sequential decrease in revenue and earnings for FQ4 2025, attributing this muted outlook primarily to uncertainties in its China business. The company cited three main factors: digestion of capacity following increased investments, a large backlog of pending export license applications with a conservative assumption of zero approvals, and non-linear demand from leading-edge customers. This is expected to result in FQ4 total revenue of approximately $6.7 billion (±$500 million) and non-GAAP EPS of ~$2.11 (±$0.20), with China's contribution to revenue decreasing to around 29%.

Analysts believe this is primarily due to China spending digestion following strong spending in 2023/2024. The just-reported July-quarter saw China revenue up 44% Q/Q, suggesting a potential pull-forward from the second half of the year. Jefferies notes that China is expected to stay at muted levels for several quarters, digesting the significant over-shipment and low utilization of tools from ‘23/’24 and resetting the base lower, suggesting a significant headwind to WFE growth into 2026.

Leading-Edge Demand Dynamics: A Secondary Factor

Driven by greater customer concentration and nonlinear demand trends, Applied also guided leading-edge logic to decline in the October quarter. The company continues to have high confidence in increasing leading-edge spending into next year, but the timing of spending appears delayed relative to prior expectations. Jefferies notes that expectations were set too high with Leading Edge guided to grow sequentially, which is now more lumpy given the concentration in TSMC and the timing of fab ramps. While the 2nm TAM and overall ramp should remain intact, tariff uncertainty and shorter visibility make it hard to predict when that will re-accelerate.

Detailed Sell-Side Theses on the Decreased Outlook

  • BofA Securities: Downgraded to Neutral, highlighting several risks. They note that slower growth could be perceived as market share loss, with their estimates suggesting four consecutive years of industry undergrowth for AMAT's Semi Systems segment. Their analysis suggests AMAT lost ~200bps in SAM share in 2024, a trend they believe is continuing. They also note that inventory days at semicap OEMs remain high, presenting a headwind for subsystem suppliers.
  • Jefferies: The outlook takes a step back with China taking a structural step down after 2 years of overspending and Leading Edge looking less reliable on timing. The ~$700M Q/Q revenue decline is attributed to a $500M drop from China ICAPS and $500M from GAA, offset by increases in display and non-China ICAPS. They see the structural reset to earnings power coming from China, with peers like KLAC and LRCX likely delayed in seeing similar trends.
  • J.P. Morgan: Maintained an Overweight rating, viewing the softness as a timing issue. The ~$700M Q/Q revenue decline is attributed to $500M lower revenues in both China and leading-edge foundry/logic, partially offset by a $300M increase in non-China ICAPS. They expect China revenue to be down 15-20% YoY over the next several quarters. However, they remain confident that 2nm GAA foundry/logic spend will pick up towards the end of 2025. They also see a path for the advanced packaging/HBM business to drive $3B+ in revenues over the next few years.
  • Goldman Sachs: Noted that while quarterly results were above the Street, the forward outlook was more conservative. They saw the sequential decline in China revenue as less of a surprise, as management had previously indicated a normalization from high 2023/24 spending levels. They also highlighted that while gross margins were strong, management believes they can hold the 48% mark going forward given a more normal China revenue mix.
